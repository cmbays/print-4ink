# User Interview Questions — [VERTICAL NAME]

**Interviewee**: [NAME / ROLE]
**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Date**: [DATE]

---

## Instructions

Adapt these questions to the specific vertical. Not every question will apply — skip irrelevant ones and follow up on interesting threads. The goal is to understand:

1. **How they work today** (workflow, tools, frequency)
2. **What hurts** (friction, time waste, frustration)
3. **What they wish for** (features, speed, simplicity)
4. **How this connects** (to other parts of their business)
5. **What success looks like** (measurable improvements)

---

## Section 1: Current Workflow (8-10 questions)

**Warm-up**: Get them talking about their typical day.

1. Walk me through how you currently handle [VERTICAL TASK] from start to finish.
2. How often do you do this? (daily / weekly / per job)
3. How long does a typical [VERTICAL TASK] take?
4. What tools do you use? (software, spreadsheets, phone, paper)
5. Who else is involved in this process?
6. What's the first thing you do when a new [VERTICAL REQUEST] comes in?
7. What's the last step before [VERTICAL TASK] is considered done?
8. Do you have a simple version and a complex version of this task? What makes one complex?
9. How do you keep track of all your [VERTICAL ITEMS]? (tracking system, email, memory)
10. What happens when something goes wrong in this process?

---

## Section 2: Pain Points (5-8 questions)

**Probe for severity**: How often? How painful on 1-10? Any workaround?

1. What's the single most frustrating thing about [VERTICAL TASK] today?
2. Where do you lose the most time?
3. What makes you say "this should be easier"?
4. Are there steps you have to do that feel unnecessary?
5. Has [COMPETITOR] ever lost your data or caused you to redo work?
6. What's the worst experience you've had with [VERTICAL TASK]?
7. Are there things you've stopped trying to do because the tool makes it too hard?
8. If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?

---

## Section 3: Desired Features (5-8 questions)

**Probe for specifics**: What would it look like? How would it save time?

1. What feature would save you the most time?
2. Is there anything [COMPETITOR] does well that you'd want to keep?
3. What would make this process feel effortless?
4. Would you want your customers to be able to [SELF-SERVICE TASK]? Under what conditions?
5. Do you ever need to reuse or duplicate past [VERTICAL ITEMS]?
6. What information do you wish you had at a glance?
7. How do you want to be notified when [VERTICAL EVENT] happens?
8. Are there things you currently do manually that you'd like automated?

---

## Section 4: Interconnections (3-5 questions)

**Probe for how this vertical connects to the rest of the business.**

1. After [VERTICAL TASK] is done, what happens next?
2. What other tools or systems does this feed into?
3. Do you need to share [VERTICAL OUTPUT] with anyone else?
4. How does [VERTICAL] connect to [ADJACENT VERTICAL — e.g., invoicing, customers]?
5. Is there data from [VERTICAL] that you wish you could see in reports?

---

## Section 5: Success Criteria (3-5 questions)

**Probe for measurable outcomes.**

1. How would you know the new [VERTICAL] is better than what you have?
2. If this was perfect, how long should [VERTICAL TASK] take?
3. What words would you use to describe the ideal experience?
4. Is there a tool (in any industry) whose experience you'd want us to match?
5. On a scale of 1-10, how important is [VERTICAL] compared to other parts of your business?
